وَإِن جَنَحُوا لِلسَّلْمِ فَاجْنَحْ لَهَا وَتَوَكَّلْ عَلَى اللَّهِ

📖 Quran 8:61: "And if they incline to peace, then incline to it and put your trust in Allah."

This verse commands believers to accept genuine offers of peace. However, the Quran also warns about those who claim peace while intending corruption:

وَإِذَا قِيلَ لَهُمْ لَا تُفْسِدُوا فِي الْأَرْضِ قَالُوا إِنَّمَا نَحْنُ مُصْلِحُونَ

📖 Quran 2:11-12: "And when it is said to them, 'Do not cause corruption on the earth,' they say, 'We are only reformers.' Unquestionably, it is they who are the corrupters, but they do not perceive."

كُلَّمَا أَوْقَدُوا نَارًا لِّلْحَرْبِ أَطْفَأَهَا اللَّهُ ۚ وَيَسْعَوْنَ فِي الْأَرْضِ فَسَادًا وَاللَّهُ لَا يُحِبُّ الْمُفْسِدِينَ

📖 Quran 5:64: "Whenever they kindle the fire of war, Allah extinguishes it. And they strive to cause corruption on the earth. And Allah does not like corrupters."

💡 The Nuance: Islam commands believers to accept genuine peace offers (8:61). But it also warns that some people use "peace" as a cover for continued aggression and corruption (2:11-12, 5:64).

📜 Prophetic Application: The Prophet (ﷺ) applied this principle at Hudaybiyyah — he accepted the treaty, but when the Quraysh broke it, he conquered Mecca. He did not accept "peace" that was merely a tactic to regroup and attack.

🌍 Modern Application: This is critical for understanding conflicts like Palestine. When oppressors repeatedly break ceasefires while continuing settlements and genocide, their "peace offers" are not genuine. Muslims are not obligated to accept deceptive peace.

وَأَعِدُّوا لَهُم مَّا اسْتَطَعْتُم مِّن قُوَّةٍ

🕌 Islamic Perspectives on Modern Conflicts

Islam provides a framework for understanding conflict and peace:

  • Justice ('Adl): "O you who have believed, be persistently standing firm for Allah, witnesses in justice." (Quran 4:135)
  • Prohibition of Aggression: "Do not transgress. Indeed, Allah does not like transgressors." (Quran 2:190)
  • Defense of the Oppressed: "And what is [the matter] with you that you do not fight in the cause of Allah and for the oppressed?" (Quran 4:75)
  • Peace as the Goal: "If they incline to peace, then incline to it." (Quran 8:61)

Modern conflicts must be analyzed through both strategic and ethical lenses. Game theory helps us understand the dynamics of conflict; Islamic ethics guides us toward just resolutions.
