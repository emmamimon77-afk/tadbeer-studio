🏰 Battle of Khaybar (7 AH / 628 CE)

The Campaign Against Jewish Strongholds • Ali's Legendary Strength

وَأَعِدُّوا لَهُم مَّا اسْتَطَعْتُم مِّن قُوَّةٍ

📖 Quran 8:60: "And prepare against them whatever you are able of power."

This verse commands believers to be in a constant state of readiness — a principle the Muslims applied at Khaybar.

📜 Historical Background

The Cause

After the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah (628 CE), the Prophet (ﷺ) turned his attention to Khaybar, a Jewish stronghold north of Medina. The Jews of Khaybar had been conspiring with the Quraysh and Ghatafan tribes against the Muslims.

Khaybar was a fertile oasis with several well-fortified forts. The Jews had assembled a force of 10,000 men, but their Ghatafan allies failed to arrive as expected.

The Muslim Army

Approximately 1,400-1,600 men, including 200 cavalry.

Key Figures

  • Muslim Commanders: Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ), Ali ibn Abi Talib, Abu Bakr, Umar
  • Jewish Commanders: Marhab (killed), Kinana ibn al-Rabi (executed for hiding treasure)

⚔️ Course of the Battle

The Muslims besieged the forts of Khaybar one by one. After several days of fighting, the Prophet (ﷺ) famously declared:

"Tomorrow I will give the standard to a man who loves Allah and His Messenger, and whom Allah and His Messenger love. He will not return until Allah grants victory at his hands."

The next morning, the Prophet (ﷺ) called Ali ibn Abi Talib, who was suffering from an eye ailment. The Prophet (ﷺ) applied his saliva to Ali's eyes, and they were instantly healed. Ali then led the final assault, killing the Jewish champion Marhab and capturing the fort of Qamus.

🎯 Key Strategic Insights

📌 Siege Warfare Tactics

The Muslims used a systematic approach: besieging each fort individually rather than trying to take them all at once. This prevented the Jews from reinforcing each other and allowed the Muslims to concentrate their forces.

📌 Leadership Selection

The Prophet's (ﷺ) public declaration about the standard-bearer created intense motivation. When Ali was chosen, his reputation for bravery inspired the entire army.

📌 Psychological Warfare

The Muslim victory at the first fort (Naim) demoralized the defenders of the remaining forts, making them more willing to negotiate surrender terms.

🎮 Game Theory Analysis

Khaybar was a classic sequential game. The Muslims attacked the weakest forts first, building momentum and demoralizing the enemy. Each victory reduced the defenders' willingness to fight.

Key strategic insights:

  • Divide and conquer: Isolate each fort to prevent coordinated resistance
  • Reputation building: Victories create a reputation that makes future opponents more likely to surrender
  • Credible commitment: The Muslims' willingness to fight to the death made their threats credible

🕌 Islamic Perspectives

إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ الَّذِينَ يُقَاتِلُونَ فِي سَبِيلِهِ صَفًّا

Quran 61:4: "Indeed, Allah loves those who fight in His cause in a row as though they are a [single] structure joined firmly."

The systematic siege of Khaybar's forts demonstrated this principle — the Muslims fought as a united force, not as individuals.

💡 Ali's Healing: A Prophetic Miracle

The healing of Ali's eyes is considered one of the Prophet's (ﷺ) miracles. It reinforced Ali's status among the companions and boosted Muslim morale before the final assault.

📜 Aftermath

The Jews of Khaybar surrendered and were allowed to remain on the condition that they give half their produce to the Muslims. This became a precedent for future treaties with conquered peoples.

The wealth obtained from Khaybar significantly strengthened the Muslim community economically. It also demonstrated the Prophet's (ﷺ) strategic wisdom in timing the campaign after the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, which had neutralized the Quraysh threat.
