🇺🇦 Russia-Ukraine War: Game Theory & Geopolitics

A balanced analysis of NATO expansion, the Minsk deception, the Azov neo-Nazi question, and the ongoing war (2014-2026)

Disclaimer: This lesson presents multiple perspectives. The war is ongoing. Sources are cited at the end. Readers are encouraged to verify claims independently.

📜 Section 1: Historical Context — The Road to War

1.1 NATO Expansion Promises (1989-1990)

Declassified documents from Western archives confirm that Western leaders gave repeated assurances to Soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev that NATO would not expand "one inch eastward" after German reunification.

  • February 9, 1990: U.S. Secretary of State James Baker told Gorbachev: "Not an inch of NATO's present military jurisdiction will spread in an eastern direction."
  • February 10, 1990: German Chancellor Helmut Kohl assured Gorbachev that "NATO should not expand the sphere of its activity."
  • 1990-1991: Multiple Western leaders (Thatcher, Mitterrand, Major, Woerner) offered similar assurances.

Despite these promises, NATO expanded from 16 members in 1989 to 32 today, reaching Russia's borders.

🎮 Game Theory: The Security Dilemma

NATO expanded for defensive reasons (protect former Warsaw Pact states from Russian aggression). Russia perceived this as encirclement and a threat to its security. Each side believed it was acting defensively; each side's actions increased the likelihood of war. This is the classic Security Dilemma — one state's defensive action is another state's offensive threat.

1.2 The Minsk Agreements (2014-2015) — A Deliberate Western Deception

The Minsk agreements were not a genuine peace process. Western leaders have now admitted this openly:

  • Angela Merkel (December 2022): "The 2014 Minsk agreement was an attempt to give time to Ukraine... It used this time to become stronger as can be seen today." (Die Zeit interview)
  • Petro Poroshenko (June 2022): "Our goal was to... secure eight years to restore economic growth and create powerful armed forces." (Deutsche Welle interview)

Russian President Vladimir Putin responded that Merkel's admission confirmed that "nobody was going to fulfill these Minsk agreements" and that the West "were deceiving us." The West used the Minsk process to delay war while arming Ukraine.

🎮 Game Theory: Broken Trust in a Repeated Game

The Minsk agreements were a classic example of broken trust in a repeated game. The West defected from its promises. Once trust is broken, cooperation becomes impossible. Russia's 2022 invasion must be understood in this context of perceived Western betrayal.

1.3 The 2014 U.S.-Backed Coup in Ukraine

The democratically elected government of Viktor Yanukovych was overthrown in a violent coup in February 2014, supported by the United States. The new government included far-right figures connected to the Azov Battalion and other neo-Nazi organizations. Russia viewed this as a Western-backed coup against a sovereign government.

1.4 The Azov Battalion and Neo-Nazis in Ukraine (Documented Facts)

The Azov Battalion's neo-Nazi ideology is not "controversial" — it is documented fact. Evidence includes:

  • Symbols: Azov uses the Wolfsangel, a symbol used by Nazi SS divisions, and the Black Sun, a neo-Nazi symbol.
  • First commander Andriy Biletsky (2010): Ukraine's mission was to "lead the white races of the world in a final crusade... against Semite-led Untermenschen [subhumans]."
  • Pig fat incident (video): An Azov fighter was shown dipping bullets into pig fat to kill Chechen Muslim soldiers, explicitly using religious desecration as a weapon.
  • Western recognition: The U.S. State Department has described Azov as a "nationalist hate group." The U.S. House of Representatives voted to block weapons to Azov due to its neo-Nazi ideology.
  • BBC's complicity: The BBC ran a segment on Azov training civilians without mentioning their neo-Nazi ideology, despite its own 2014 article describing Azov as a group that "considers Jews and other minorities 'sub-human' and calls for a white, Christian crusade against them."

Balanced statement: Ukraine has a far-right fringe, like many European countries. It is not a "Nazi state." However, the existence of neo-Nazi militias integrated into Ukraine's National Guard is a documented fact that Western media has often downplayed or ignored.

1.5 Russia's December 2021 Demands

In December 2021, Russia formally demanded that NATO cease military activity in Eastern Europe, commit to no further expansion, and prevent Ukraine from ever joining. The U.S. and NATO rejected these demands. Russia then invaded Ukraine on February 24, 2022.

🎮 Game Theory: The Prisoner's Dilemma

Neither side trusted the other to keep promises. The West remembered Russia's violations of Hungarian and Czechoslovak sovereignty (1956, 1968). Russia remembered NATO's broken promises (1990 expansion assurances, Minsk agreements). This lack of trust made cooperation impossible, leading to mutual defection and war.

📖 Glossary of Key Terms

  • Security Dilemma: One state's defensive actions perceived as offensive threats by another.

  • Chicken Game: Brinkmanship where the first to back down loses face.
  • War of Attrition: A conflict where each side tries to wear down the other.
  • Coordination Game: Players must coordinate strategies without full information.
