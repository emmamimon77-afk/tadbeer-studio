📜 Section 1: Historical Context — The Road to War

1.1 NATO Expansion Promises (1989-1990)

Declassified documents from Western archives confirm that Western leaders gave repeated assurances to Soviet leader Mikhail Gorbachev that NATO would not expand "one inch eastward" after German reunification.

  • February 9, 1990: U.S. Secretary of State James Baker told Gorbachev: "Not an inch of NATO's present military jurisdiction will spread in an eastern direction."
  • February 10, 1990: German Chancellor Helmut Kohl assured Gorbachev that "NATO should not expand the sphere of its activity."
  • 1990-1991: Multiple Western leaders (Thatcher, Mitterrand, Major, Woerner) offered similar assurances.

Despite these promises, NATO expanded from 16 members in 1989 to 32 today, reaching Russia's borders.

🎮 Game Theory: The Security Dilemma

NATO expanded for defensive reasons (protect former Warsaw Pact states from Russian aggression). Russia perceived this as encirclement and a threat to its security. Each side believed it was acting defensively; each side's actions increased the likelihood of war. This is the classic Security Dilemma — one state's defensive action is another state's offensive threat.

1.2 The Minsk Agreements (2014-2015) — A Deliberate Western Deception

The Minsk agreements were not a genuine peace process. Western leaders have now admitted this openly:

  • Petro Poroshenko (June 2022): "Our goal was to... secure eight years to restore economic growth and create powerful armed forces." (Deutsche Welle interview)

Russian President Vladimir Putin responded that Merkel's admission confirmed that "nobody was going to fulfill these Minsk agreements" and that the West "were deceiving us." The West used the Minsk process to delay war while arming Ukraine.
