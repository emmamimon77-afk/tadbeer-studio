🇹🇼 Taiwan Strait Crisis

US-China strategic competition, the One-China principle, and the Chicken Game in the South China Sea

وَإِن جَنَحُوا لِلسَّلْمِ فَاجْنَحْ لَهَا

📖 Quran 8:61: "If they incline to peace, then incline to it."

This verse reminds all parties that peace should always be the goal, even in tense strategic standoffs.

🎯 Key Concepts

🇨🇳 The One-China Principle

China's position: Taiwan is an inalienable part of China. No independence, no official diplomatic relations with countries that recognize Taiwan as separate. This is recognized by the UN and most nations.

🇺🇸 The US Position

The US "acknowledges" the One-China policy but maintains unofficial relations with Taiwan, sells weapons, and has a statutory obligation to help Taiwan defend itself (Taiwan Relations Act).

🎮 Game Theory Analysis: Chicken Game

The Taiwan Strait is a classic Chicken Game:

  • China has pre-committed to reunification — backing down would mean loss of face and legitimacy
  • The US has pre-committed to Taiwan's defense — backing down would undermine alliance credibility
  • Neither can swerve without losing face
  • Mutual straight driving (war) would be catastrophic for both economies and the world

The key question: Are both sides' commitments credible? Would the US really go to war over Taiwan? Would China really risk economic devastation?

💰 Economic Interdependence

China and the US are deeply economically intertwined. Taiwan is critical to the global semiconductor supply chain (TSMC produces over 90% of advanced chips). A war would destroy global tech industries.

🕌 Islamic Perspectives

وَأَعِدُّوا لَهُم مَّا اسْتَطَعْتُم مِّن قُوَّةٍ

Quran 8:60: "And prepare against them whatever you are able of power."

Both China and the US are following this principle — preparing military power as a deterrent. However, Islam also commands peace when possible (8:61).

🕌 China's Muslim Population

China has over 20 million Muslims (Uyghurs, Hui, Kazakhs, etc.). Their rights and treatment are relevant to Islamic perspectives on the conflict. Any war would impact these communities.
