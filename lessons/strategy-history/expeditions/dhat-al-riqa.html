📜 Expedition of Dhat al-Riqa — 4 AH / 625 CE

Campaign Against the Ghatafan Tribes • Demonstrating Muslim Strength

وَأَعِدُّوا لَهُم مَّا اسْتَطَعْتُم مِّن قُوَّةٍ

📖 Quran 8:60: "And prepare against them whatever you are able of power."

This expedition demonstrated the principle of projecting strength to deter potential aggressors.

📜 Historical Background

The Cause

The Ghatafan tribes were nomadic groups living northeast of Medina. They had been involved in the Confederate army during the Battle of the Trench (5 AH) and posed a potential threat to Medina's northern flank.

The expedition aimed to demonstrate Muslim strength and deter future attacks.

The Name "Dhat al-Riqa"

"Dhat al-Riqa" means "the place of rags" — named because the Muslim soldiers' feet became wrapped in rags during the difficult journey through rocky terrain.

⚔️ Course of the Expedition

The Prophet (ﷺ) led 400-800 men on this expedition. The Ghatafan, hearing of the Muslim approach, fled into the mountains, leaving behind their livestock and possessions.

There was no major battle. The Muslims demonstrated their strength, and the Ghatafan tribes chose to avoid confrontation.

During this expedition, the prayer of fear (Salat al-Khawf) was established — a shortened prayer for soldiers in active combat zones.

🎯 Key Strategic Insights

📌 Deterrence Through Presence

The mere presence of the Muslim army was enough to deter the Ghatafan from attacking. This is a classic example of deterrence — preventing conflict through credible strength.

📌 The Prayer of Fear

The establishment of Salat al-Khawf shows Islam's practicality — even in war, prayer is maintained, but in a form adapted to the circumstances.

🎮 Game Theory Analysis

This expedition was a classic signaling game. The Muslims sent a costly signal (marching through difficult terrain) to demonstrate their resolve. The Ghatafan, seeing this credible signal, chose to flee rather than fight.
