📜 Expedition of Banu Nadir — 4 AH / 625 CE

The Second Jewish Tribe Expelled • Pre-emptive Action Against Conspiracy

هُوَ الَّذِي أَخْرَجَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا مِنْ أَهْلِ الْكِتَابِ مِن دِيَارِهِمْ لِأَوَّلِ الْحَشْرِ

📖 Quran 59:2: "He is the one who expelled those who disbelieved among the People of the Book from their homes at the first gathering."

This verse refers specifically to the expulsion of Banu Nadir.

📜 Historical Background

The Conspiracy

After the Battle of Uhud (3 AH), the Prophet (ﷺ) visited the Banu Nadir to seek financial assistance for the blood money of two men killed by a Muslim. The Banu Nadir agreed to help.

While the Prophet (ﷺ) was waiting against a wall, some members of Banu Nadir plotted to drop a large rock on his head. The Prophet (ﷺ) was informed of the plot by divine revelation and quickly left.

The Ultimatum

The Prophet (ﷺ) gave the Banu Nadir an ultimatum: leave Medina within ten days or face the consequences. The Banu Nadir initially agreed but then, instigated by Abd Allah ibn Ubayy (the chief hypocrite), decided to resist.

Ibn Ubayy promised them 2,000 men to support them, but when the siege began, he failed to show up.

⚔️ Course of the Expedition

The Prophet (ﷺ) besieged the Banu Nadir in their well-fortified forts for 21 days. The Banu Nadir were skilled archers and had strong fortifications, but they were cut off from supplies and had no allies.

Ibn Ubayy's promised support never materialized. The Banu Nadir realized they could not win and surrendered.

They were allowed to leave with their movable property (except weapons), taking their families and possessions on 600 camels. They destroyed their own forts before leaving.

The Banu Nadir migrated to Khaybar, where they later plotted against the Muslims again.

🎯 Key Strategic Insights

📌 Pre-emptive Action Against Conspiracy

The Prophet (ﷺ) acted pre-emptively to neutralize a threat before it could materialize. This is a legitimate principle in Islamic strategic thought — preventing harm before it occurs.

📌 Divine Revelation as Intelligence

The Prophet (ﷺ) learned of the plot through divine revelation. This is a unique aspect of his leadership — he had access to information no human intelligence could provide.

📌 The Hypocrite's Betrayal

Abd Allah ibn Ubayy's failure to support Banu Nadir exposed his unreliability. He promised help but did not deliver, revealing that his loyalty was only to himself.

🎮 Game Theory Analysis

The Banu Nadir's decision to resist was based on false information — they believed Ibn Ubayy would support them. This is a classic information asymmetry problem. They acted on unreliable information and paid the price.

Key strategic insights:

  • Pre-emptive action can prevent a threat from becoming a crisis
  • Reliance on unreliable allies is a strategic mistake
  • The Prophet's (ﷺ) intelligence advantage (divine revelation) made his pre-emptive strikes precise

🕌 Islamic Perspectives

يُخَادِعُونَ اللَّهَ وَالَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَمَا يَخْدَعُونَ إِلَّا أَنفُسَهُمْ

Quran 2:9: "They deceive Allah and those who believe, but they deceive not except themselves."

This verse describes the hypocrites who supported Banu Nadir but failed to act.

📜 Aftermath

The land and date-palm groves of Banu Nadir were distributed among the Muhajirun (emigrants), who had lost their property in Mecca. This was the first time the Muslims took land from an enemy tribe as war booty.

The Banu Nadir later became key instigators of the Battle of the Trench (5 AH), forming an alliance with the Quraysh and Ghatafan to attack Medina.
