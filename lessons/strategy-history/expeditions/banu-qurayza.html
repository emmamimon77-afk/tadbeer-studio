📜 Expedition of Banu Qurayza — 5 AH / 627 CE

Treason During Wartime • Judgment Under Their Own Law

وَأَنزَلَ الَّذِينَ ظَاهَرُوهُم مِّنْ أَهْلِ الْكِتَابِ مِن صَيَاصِيهِمْ

📖 Quran 33:26: "And He brought down those who supported them among the People of the Book from their fortresses..."

This verse refers to the Banu Qurayza, who broke their treaty during the Battle of the Trench.

📜 Historical Background

The Treason

During the Battle of the Trench (5 AH / 627 CE), the Jewish tribe of Banu Qurayza broke their treaty with the Muslims. They conspired with the Quraysh and Ghatafan confederacy to attack Medina from within.

The Banu Qurayza's forts were strategically located in the south of Medina, directly behind the Muslim lines. If they had attacked, the Muslims would have been surrounded on two fronts.

The Betrayal Uncovered

The Prophet (ﷺ) learned of the betrayal through intelligence. The confederates asked Banu Qurayza to attack the Muslim women and children, but they hesitated, demanding hostages first. This delay gave the Muslims time to respond.

After the confederates withdrew, the Prophet (ﷺ) besieged the Banu Qurayza in their forts.

⚔️ Course of the Expedition

The Muslims besieged the Banu Qurayza for 25 days. The tribe was well-fortified and had substantial provisions, but they had no allies to support them.

When the Banu Qurayza realized they could not win, they asked to negotiate. The Prophet (ﷺ) agreed to let them choose their own judge — Sa'd ibn Mu'adh, a companion who was mortally wounded in the battle and a former ally of Banu Qurayza.

Sa'd ibn Mu'adh judged them according to the Torah law of Deuteronomy 20:10-14 — death for the men of fighting age, and women and children taken as captives. The Banu Qurayza accepted the judgment, and it was carried out.

🎯 Key Strategic Insights

📌 Treason During Wartime

Breaking a treaty during active warfare is a grave offense. The Banu Qurayza's actions threatened the survival of the entire Muslim community.

📌 Judgment Under Their Own Law

The Prophet (ﷺ) allowed the Banu Qurayza to be judged by a judge of their own choosing, according to their own scripture. This demonstrates the Islamic principle of justice — even for enemies.

📌 Deterrence

The harsh punishment served as a deterrent. After Banu Qurayza, no tribe in Medina ever betrayed the Muslims again.

🎮 Game Theory Analysis

The Banu Qurayza's defection was a classic betrayal in a repeated game. They calculated that the confederates would win and that defecting would benefit them. They miscalculated.

Key strategic insights:

  • Defecting from a coalition is risky if you misjudge the balance of power
  • Credible punishment for betrayal deters future defections
  • The Prophet's (ﷺ) decision to let the Banu Qurayza choose their own judge demonstrated fairness, which enhanced his reputation

🕌 Islamic Perspectives

وَإِن نَّكَثُوا أَيْمَانَهُم مِّن بَعْدِ عَهْدِهِمْ

Quran 9:12-13: "If they break their oaths after their treaty... then fight the leaders of disbelief."

This verse establishes that breaking treaties is a justification for war.

📜 Aftermath

The elimination of Banu Qurayza removed the last major Jewish tribe from Medina. The Muslims were now the undisputed political and military power in the city.

The wealth and land of Banu Qurayza were distributed among the Muslims, further strengthening the community economically.
