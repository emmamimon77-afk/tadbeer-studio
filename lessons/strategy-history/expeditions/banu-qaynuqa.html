📜 Expedition of Banu Qaynuqa — 2 AH / 624 CE

The First Jewish Tribe Expelled • Consequences of Treaty Violation

وَأَوْفُوا بِالْعَهْدِ ۖ إِنَّ الْعَهْدَ كَانَ مَسْئُولًا

📖 Quran 17:34: "And fulfill the covenant. Indeed, the covenant will be questioned."

This verse emphasizes the importance of keeping treaties — a principle the Banu Qaynuqa violated.

📜 Historical Background

The Treaty

After the Hijra to Medina, the Prophet (ﷺ) established the Constitution of Medina, which granted religious freedom and mutual protection to all tribes, including the Jewish tribes of Banu Qaynuqa, Banu Nadir, and Banu Qurayza.

The treaty stipulated that the Jews would not aid the Quraysh against the Muslims and would defend Medina together.

The Violation

After the Battle of Badr (2 AH), the Banu Qaynuqa grew resentful. They were the wealthiest and most powerful of the Jewish tribes. In the market of Medina, a Jewish goldsmith attempted to uncover a Muslim woman's face. When a Muslim man intervened, the goldsmith killed him. The tribe then rallied to support the killer, refusing to hand him over for justice.

This was a clear violation of the treaty.

⚔️ Course of the Expedition

The Prophet (ﷺ) besieged the Banu Qaynuqa in their forts for 15 days. They were skilled warriors and well-fortified, but with no allies to support them, they surrendered.

The Prophet (ﷺ) initially wanted to execute the men for treason, but Abd Allah ibn Ubayy (the chief hypocrite) interceded on their behalf. The Prophet (ﷺ) accepted the intercession and instead expelled them from Medina. They were allowed to leave with their movable property, but their weapons and goldsmithing tools were confiscated.

The Banu Qaynuqa migrated to Syria, where they later allied with the Byzantines against the Muslims.

🎯 Key Strategic Insights

📌 Consequences of Treaty Violation

The expedition demonstrated that breaking treaties with the Muslims had severe consequences. This deterred other tribes from violating their agreements.

📌 The Hypocrite's Role

Abd Allah ibn Ubayy's intercession saved the Banu Qaynuqa from execution, but it also revealed his true allegiance. He was the leader of the hypocrites (Munafiqun) and would later cause significant internal problems for the Muslim community.

📌 Expulsion vs. Execution

The Prophet (ﷺ) chose expulsion over execution, demonstrating mercy while still enforcing justice. This balanced approach preserved the possibility of future reconciliation.

🎮 Game Theory Analysis

The Banu Qaynuqa miscalculated the Muslims' strength. After Badr, they believed the Muslims were still vulnerable. They defected from the treaty, expecting their allies to support them — but those allies did not come.

Key strategic insights:

  • Reputation matters: The Muslims' victory at Badr established their credibility as a military force
  • Deterrence works when the threat is credible: The Banu Qaynuqa violated the treaty because they did not believe the Muslims would enforce it
  • Defection from a coalition has consequences: The Banu Qaynuqa's allies abandoned them when they needed help

🕌 Islamic Perspectives

إِنَّمَا جَزَاءُ الَّذِينَ يُحَارِبُونَ اللَّهَ وَرَسُولَهُ وَيَسْعَوْنَ فِي الْأَرْضِ فَسَادًا

Quran 5:33: "The punishment of those who wage war against Allah and His Messenger and strive upon earth [to cause] corruption is execution or crucifixion or the cutting off of hands and feet..."

The Banu Qaynuqa were guilty of "waging war against Allah and His Messenger" by violating the treaty and killing a Muslim. The Prophet (ﷺ) had the right to execute them but chose mercy.

📜 Aftermath

The expulsion of Banu Qaynuqa was a warning to the other Jewish tribes. It demonstrated that the Muslims would enforce the terms of the Constitution of Medina.

The confiscated wealth and weapons strengthened the Muslim community economically and militarily.
