🤲 My Journey

Assalamu alaikum. My name is Fatimah Khin Khin Aye, and this is my humble effort to share what I have learned.

When I first encountered game theory, I was deeply intrigued. The idea that strategic decision-making could be analyzed, modeled, and applied to real-world situations fascinated me. As I researched further, I discovered how game theory has been used throughout history — in politics, economics, military strategy, and everyday life.

But then a question arose in my heart: What would the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) say about this?

This question led me down a beautiful path of discovery. I found a profound connection between game theory and the Islamic concept of Tadbeer (تدبير) — prophetic insight, foresight, and strategic planning rooted in justice, fairness, and divine guidance.

🕌 The Prophetic Connection

The Prophet (ﷺ) was not only a spiritual leader but also the greatest strategist humanity has ever known. His decisions in the Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, the Battle of the Trench, and the Conquest of Mecca demonstrate a masterful understanding of long-term thinking, patience, and the art of the "long game."

However, the Prophet's (ﷺ) approach to strategy is fundamentally different from conventional game theory. While conventional models often prioritize self-interest, profit maximization, and winning at the expense of others, the prophetic model is rooted in:

  • Justice ('Adl) — Fairness to all parties, even enemies
  • Truthfulness (Sidq) — No deception, no cheating, no betrayal
  • Patience (Sabr) — Willingness to sacrifice short-term gain for long-term victory
  • Forgiveness — Converting enemies into allies through mercy
  • Consultation (Shura) — Collective decision-making even when the leader has prior insight

📚 The Purpose of This Hub

As I learned more, I realized that this knowledge should not remain confined to academic papers or elite circles. It should be shared — freely, openly, and humbly.

This hub is my attempt to bring together:

  • Game theory analysis of historical and modern conflicts
  • Prophetic Tadbeer as a framework for strategic thinking
  • Comparative eschatology — understanding how different faiths view the end of times
  • Interactive simulators to learn through experience
  • Quizzes to test and reinforce understanding

  • Share constructive feedback to help improve this platform
